Genitive Case

The genitive case is one of six cases in Russian grammar. It is used to indicate possession or ownership, as well as to express other relationships between nouns. In Russian, the ending of a noun or pronoun in the genitive case typically depends on the gender, number, and animacy of the word.

For example, the noun "замок" (castle) is masculine and singular, so in the genitive case it would be written as "замка" (zamka). But if the noun were feminine, such as "книга" (book), it would be written as "книги" (knigi) in the genitive case.

The same rules apply to pronouns. For example, the pronoun "я" (I) is always written as "меня" (menya) in the genitive case, regardless of its gender or number.

It is important for students of Russian to understand and master the genitive case, as it is a crucial part of correct sentence structure in the language. Misusing the genitive case can lead to confusion or misunderstandings.
