Cases

In Russian, nouns, pronouns, and adjectives are declined to reflect their grammatical role in the sentence. This is known as declension, and it is accomplished through the use of cases. There are six cases in Russian: nominative, genitive, dative, accusative, instrumental, and prepositional.

The nominative case is used for the subject of a sentence. The genitive case is used to indicate possession or ownership. The dative case indicates the indirect object of a sentence, while the accusative case indicates the direct object. The instrumental case indicates the means or instrument by which something is done, and the prepositional case is used with certain prepositions.

Declension in Russian is a complex and nuanced aspect of the language, and it is important for students of Russian to understand and master it in order to speak and write correctly.
