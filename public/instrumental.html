Instrumental Case

The instrumental case is one of six cases in Russian grammar. It is used to indicate the means or instrument by which something is done, as well as to express other relationships between nouns. In Russian, the ending of a noun or pronoun in the instrumental case typically depends on the gender, number, and animacy of the word.

For example, the noun "замок" (castle) is masculine and singular, so in the instrumental case it would be written as "замком" (zamkom). But if the noun were feminine, such as "книга" (book), it would be written as "книгой" (knigoy) in the instrumental case.

The same rules apply to pronouns. For example, the pronoun "я" (I) is always written as "мной" (mnoy) in the instrumental case, regardless of its gender or number.

It is important for students of Russian to understand and master the instrumental case, as it is a crucial part of correct sentence structure in the language. Misusing the instrumental case can lead to confusion or misunderstandings.
