Pronouns

In Russian, pronouns are used to replace nouns in a sentence, just like in English. Russian has six personal pronouns, which are used to indicate the person speaking (first person), the person being spoken to (second person), or the person or thing being spoken about (third person).

These pronouns have different forms depending on their grammatical case, which is determined by their function in the sentence. Russian has six grammatical cases, which are used to indicate the role of a noun or pronoun in the sentence.

In addition to having different forms depending on their gramtical case, the pronouns must also match the gender/pluarlity in which the noun it replaces. These charts describe how the pronoun declines and matches with gender.
