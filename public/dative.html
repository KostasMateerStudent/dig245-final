Dative Case

The dative case is one of six cases in Russian grammar. It is used to indicate the indirect object of a sentence, that is, the person or thing to or for whom the action of the verb is performed. In Russian, the ending of a noun or pronoun in the dative case typically depends on the gender, number, and animacy of the word.

For example, the noun "замок" (castle) is masculine and singular, so in the dative case it would be written as "замку" (zamku). But if the noun were feminine, such as "книга" (book), it would be written as "книге" (knige) in the dative case.

The same rules apply to pronouns. For example, the pronoun "я" (I) is always written as "мне" (mne) in the dative case, regardless of its gender or number.

It is important for students of Russian to understand and master the dative case, as it is a crucial part of correct sentence structure in the language. Misusing the dative case can lead to confusion or misunderstandings.
