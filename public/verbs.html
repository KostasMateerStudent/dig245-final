Verbs

Verbs in Russian are words that express actions or states of being. They are used to indicate what the subject of a sentence is doing or what its state of being is. For example, the verb "говорить" (to speak) indicates that the subject of the sentence is speaking, and the verb "быть" (to be) indicates that the subject of the sentence is in a particular state of being. Verbs in Russian have different forms that indicate the person, number, tense, and aspect of the action or state of being that they express. For example, the verb "говорить" has the following forms: "говорю" (I speak), "говоришь" (you speak), "говорит" (he/she speaks), "говорим" (we speak), "говорите" (you speak), "говорят" (they speak).
